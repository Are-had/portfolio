About Me

My journey from Electronics to Intelligent Systems.

Hello, I am Arezki, a 23-year-old engineering student passionate about the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Applied Physics. Currently, I am completing my Master 2 in Automatic Control and Robotics, specializing in Intelligent Systems at Sorbonne University (Paris).

My academic journey is built on a progressive and rigorous foundation. It began in Algeria with two intensive years at IGEE (ex-INELEC), where I developed a strong intuition for hardware and electronics. In 2022, I moved to France to join Sorbonne University, where I completed my Licence in Electronics, Energy, and Automation (EEA). This step allowed me to solidify my theoretical understanding of physical systems before advancing to my current Master's degree, where I now focus on bridging the gap between physical constraints and advanced AI algorithms.

I am currently applying this dual expertise as a Research Apprentice at the Laboratoire de Météorologie Dynamique (LMD). I am actively contributing to the EarthCARE space mission, a major collaboration between ESA and JAXA. My work focuses on the geophysical validation of the ATLID satellite instrument by comparing its data with ground-based observations from the IPRAL lidar at the SIRTA atmospheric observatory.

Beyond atmospheric physics, my academic training allows me to master advanced Deep Learning architectures and Signal Processing techniques (Image & Audio), which I apply to various complex systems. I am now seeking a PhD position or an R&D opportunity to continue solving scientific challenges using intelligent systems.

Bridging Physics and AI

"Through my research experience at LMD (Laboratoire de Météorologie Dynamique), I learned that real-world data is messy. Whether I am validating satellite lidar data from the EarthCARE mission or designing a control loop, I prioritize physical interpretability over 'black-box' solutions. I am driven by the desire to solve complex problems where physics and algorithms intersect."
